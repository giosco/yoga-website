About Me

My journey, philosophy, and approach to yoga

My Yoga Journey

My path to becoming a yoga instructor began over 10 years ago when I discovered yoga as a way to manage stress and find balance in my busy life. What started as a personal practice soon evolved into a passion for sharing the transformative benefits of yoga with others.

After completing my 200-hour Yoga Teacher Training in Hatha Yoga, I continued my education with specialized training in Vinyasa, Yin, and Restorative yoga. I've also studied yoga philosophy, anatomy, and meditation to provide a well-rounded approach to my teaching.

My teaching style combines traditional yoga principles with modern understanding of the body and mind, creating a practice that is both authentic and accessible to contemporary practitioners.

Certifications

  • 200-Hour Yoga Teacher Training (Yoga Alliance Certified)
  • 50-Hour Yin Yoga Training
  • Restorative Yoga Certification
  • Prenatal Yoga Certification
  • Meditation and Mindfulness Training

My Yoga Philosophy

Bringing mindfulness, balance, and accessibility to every practice

Compassionate Approach

I believe yoga should be practiced with kindness toward oneself. My teaching emphasizes self-acceptance and meeting yourself where you are each day, without judgment or competition.

Personalized Practice

Every body is unique, and so should be every yoga practice. I tailor each session to the individual needs, goals, and limitations of my students, ensuring a safe and effective practice.

Holistic Balance

Yoga is more than physical postures. I integrate breath work, meditation, and mindfulness into each session to nurture not just the body, but also the mind and spirit.

Accessibility

I am committed to making yoga accessible to everyone, regardless of age, body type, or experience level. My sessions are designed to be inclusive and adaptable to diverse needs.

Teaching Approach

How I bring yoga to you in your preferred environment
